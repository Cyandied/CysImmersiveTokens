So what is an alpha?

The alpha is the very heart of this whole oparation, it is the element that matters. It is what chnages colors, thus making the token "immersive".

How do I make an alpha?

The alpha (or alphas if you so choose) is a transparent, most conviently uniformly colored, part. It lays just beneath your lineart and shading (and possibly static colors, but that is not important now). It is the part of the image you can change the color of, and needs to be marked in the editor as an alpha layer, then the color picker will activate, and you can marvel at my costum made loading bar while waiting for the (at times) lenthy process of replacing the color on your alpha. When replacing the color of your aplha, the editor will totally cover the original coloring, so any shading, highlight, texture, or pattern In your drawing program with will make your alpha with two methods, depending on if you have a premade flat token, or you alreayd have a layer separated token.

I already have a flat image I want to "immersify", how do I separate the alpha(s)?

  1. Open your token in your preferred art or image editing software, the important part is that the software can work with diffrent layers.
  2. Start by getting an overview of your token. What parts of the token do you want to be color changeing? What parts should remain static?
  3. Grab the polygon selection tool and go around the alpha you have decided to cut out, if you have several alphas you wish to stack, select the bottom most one first if it encompasses the rest.
  4. When you have selected your alpha, copy the selection, and paste it on a layer above.
  5. Repeat the process for every alpha you want.
  6. For best results, add a gaussian blur to the alpha layers, about 1 to 2 pixels, and duplicate them a few times (merging them after to get one layer per alpha).
  7. Save each layer as an image by itself, make it a transparent png or a webp. You will have some alphas and the original image.
  8. Upload the images to the editor, the alpahs should be on top of the original image. Choose colors to your heart's desire!

I have an image separated in layers, how do I separate the alpha(s)?

  1. Ensure that the parts you wish to be alpahs are on a layer, completely by themsleves.
  2. Save each layer as a seperate image, make it a transparent png or a webp. You will have some alphas, and some static graphics.
  3. Upload the images to the editor. If you need to you can take a screenshot of the layer separation in your image editing software, use this to reference when assinging a layer in the editor.

Tips and tricks

  • Never pick extremely dark or extremely light colors for your tokens, this often eats a lot of detail and focus.
  • Similiarily, dont choose colors that are extremely saturated, this can burn the eyes of you and your players.
  • Keeping the biggest alpha intact and not cutting other alpahs from it can allow you to use a multi-alpha token as a mono-alpha token, avoiding having to choose the same color multiple times by not importing the smaller alphas.
